Elena Mengual Breton

"If you like it and you are passionate about it, you will get what you want."

Introduction

Dr. Elena Mengual Bretón, current teacher of the subject of mathematics at the Salesianos San Bernardo school in Huesca, Aragón, Spain. A woman specialized in the Math field of STEM, with a great mind and perseverance so that her students can understand and see the magic behind mathematics. At school she enjoys teaching classes a lot, and motivating her students every day, such as encouraging them to participate in math Olympics or seeing the logic behind daily events. She is also in charge of the pastoral office of the school, thus she thinks about all the activities that are celebrated in festivities in the center. She is 30 years old, was born on April 20, 1989 and studied her career at the University of Zaragoza. His doctorate was in Characterization of the mathematical knowledge underlying the primary school textbook in measure and was carried out at the Autonomous University of Barcelona.

Life and Career

Childhood and school years

Her childhood defines it as normal, but complicated like that of all adolescents, with the typical desire of wanting to try new experiences, wanting to know who you are, what you want to dedicate yourself to, what will become of your life. She studied at the school where she currently teaches, Salesianos de Huesca until fourth year of secondary education in the Spanish educational system and, for high school, she went to the Altoaragón center, also located in Huesca. It is curious that since childhood she was already curious about mathematics, and in the last years of secondary education she commented on the idea of studying mathematics to her teacher, Fernando Lafuente, with which she finally decided and was clear about it.

About the School

Salesianos Huesca is a center located on Avenida Monreal in this city. It has innovative facilities to treat its students in the best possible way, from kindergarten to secondary education, through primary education. They have a new method, in which students are connected to the internet through their own laptops and tablets, and work cooperatively to learn to be a person.
